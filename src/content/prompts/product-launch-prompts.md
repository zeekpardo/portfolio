prompt: |
  ## 1. Marketing Page Copy

  I'm launching [product name] - [one-sentence description] for [audience].

  Here are real quotes from the community describing the problem:
  [Paste 5-10 quotes]

  Here are the key features:
  [List features]

  Pricing: [details]
  Platform: [details]

  Write the full landing page copy:
  1. Hero section with headline, subheading, and CTA
  2. "Sound familiar?" section using the community's own pain points (paraphrased, not quoted directly)
  3. Feature sections (lead with the problem each feature solves, not the feature itself)
  4. Pricing section
  5. FAQ (anticipate the 8 most common questions)
  6. Final CTA

  Tone: A maker talking to other makers. Not corporate. Not salesy. Direct, confident, specific.
  Do not lead with pricing or "free" - lead with the problem being solved.

  ## 2. Community Launch Posts

  I'm launching [product] in these communities:
  1. [Community 1 - e.g., r/lasercutting]
  2. [Community 2 - e.g., Facebook group name]
  3. [Community 3 - e.g., specific forum]

  Important context:
  - Most of these communities don't allow self-promotion
  - I need to lead with the problem and ask for testers, not announce a product
  - I should NOT include links in the post - let people ask in comments/DMs
  - I should NOT mention pricing in the post
  - Lead with the problem, not the product

  For each community, write:
  1. A post title that describes the PROBLEM (not the product)
  2. A post body that shares my frustration, mentions I built something, and asks for testers
  3. Customize the language and specific pain points for each community's focus

  Also write 5 comment templates I can use to reply when I see people discussing this problem organically. These should feel like a peer sharing something helpful, not a company promoting a product.

  ## 3. Video Script (60 seconds)

  Write a 60-second video script for [product].

  Structure:
  - 0-3 seconds: Hook (show the problem visually)
  - 3-10 seconds: Show the mess (screen recording of the current painful state)
  - 10-18 seconds: Describe the pain (use community language)
  - 18-35 seconds: Show the solution (screen recording of key workflow)
  - 35-48 seconds: Show the killer feature (the one unique thing)
  - 48-60 seconds: CTA (where to find it - no pricing)

  Keep it conversational. No marketing language. No "revolutionary" or "game-changing."

  ## 4. Influencer Outreach

  I'm reaching out to content creators who serve [audience] to offer them a free license for [product].

  Write a short outreach email/DM that:
  1. Shows I know their content (I'll customize the first line)
  2. Explains what the product does in one sentence
  3. Explains why their audience would care
  4. Offers a free license with no obligation
  5. Includes a link to a 60-second demo
  6. Is under 100 words total

  Keep it casual and genuine - not a pitch deck.
